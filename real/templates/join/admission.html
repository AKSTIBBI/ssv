At Shri Siddhi Vinayak Educational Trust, we believe in providing quality education that focuses on the all-round development of students. Our admission process is designed to identify potential students who would benefit from our holistic approach to education and would contribute positively to our school community.

We welcome applications from students of all backgrounds and abilities who demonstrate a desire to learn and grow in a nurturing environment.

Admission Process Steps

1

Submit Application

Fill out the admission form (online or offline) with all required details and documents.

2

Document Verification

Submit original documents for verification along with the application form.

3

Entrance Assessment

Students seeking admission to classes 2-12 may need to take an entrance assessment.

4

Interview

A brief interaction with the student and parents to understand expectations and goals.

5

Admission Confirmation

Successful candidates will receive an admission confirmation letter.

6

Fee Payment

Complete the admission process by paying the required fees within the stipulated time.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is there an entrance test for admission?

For Nursery to Class 1, there is no formal entrance test. For Classes 2 and above, there is a basic assessment to evaluate the student's current academic level, primarily to understand any learning gaps that may need to be addressed.

What is the medium of instruction?

We offer both English and Hindi mediums of instruction. Parents can choose the preferred medium at the time of admission based on their child's comfort and learning needs.

Is there any transportation facility available?

Yes, the school provides transportation services covering a radius of 25 km around Tibbi. The bus fee is separate from the tuition fee and varies based on the distance from the school.

What is the school's policy on admissions mid-session?

Mid-session admissions are considered on a case-by-case basis, subject to seat availability and valid reasons for transfer. Additional assessments may be required to ensure the student can adapt to the ongoing curriculum.

Are there any reservations under the RTE Act?

Yes, as per the Right to Education (RTE) Act, we reserve 25% of seats at the entry level for children from economically weaker sections and disadvantaged groups. For more information about RTE admissions, please visit our RTE section.
