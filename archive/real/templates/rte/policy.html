Right to Education (RTE) Policy

Admission Policy under RTE Act

In accordance with the Right to Education Act, 2009, Shri Siddhi Vinayak Educational Trust has formulated the following policy for admissions under the RTE quota:

1. Reservation of Seats

The trust reserves 25% of the total seats in the entry-level class (Nursery/Class 1) for children belonging to disadvantaged groups and economically weaker sections of society. These seats are offered free of cost, with no fees charged for admission, tuition, or any other charges throughout the elementary education period.

2. Eligibility Criteria

The following categories of children are eligible for admission under the RTE quota:

  • Children belonging to economically weaker sections (annual family income less than Rs. 8 lakhs)
  • Children belonging to disadvantaged groups (SC/ST/OBC-Non-Creamy Layer)
  • Children with special needs/disabilities
  • Orphans and children in need of care and protection

The child must be between 6-14 years of age for classes 1-8 (age-appropriate for the concerned class). For entry-level admission (Nursery), the child must be at least 3 years of age as of March 31 of the admission year.

3. Required Documents

The following documents are required to be submitted along with the RTE admission application:

  • Birth Certificate of the child
  • Proof of residence (Aadhaar Card, Voter ID, Electricity Bill, etc.)
  • Income Certificate issued by a competent authority (for EWS category)
  • Caste Certificate issued by a competent authority (for SC/ST/OBC-NCL categories)
  • Disability Certificate (if applicable)
  • Recent passport-sized photographs of the child and parents/guardians
  • Any other document as specified by the government/education department

4. Admission Process

  1. Application forms for RTE admissions are made available at the school office and on the school website as per the schedule announced by the education department.
  2. Completed application forms with all required documents must be submitted to the school office within the specified timeline.
  3. All applications are scrutinized for eligibility as per RTE guidelines.
  4. If the number of eligible applications exceeds the available seats, selection is done through a transparent lottery system in the presence of parents and education department representatives.
  5. Selected candidates are required to complete the admission formalities within the stipulated time frame.

5. No Screening Process

In accordance with the RTE Act, the school does not conduct any form of screening or testing for children or their parents during the admission process. Admissions are granted solely based on the eligibility criteria and the lottery system (if required).

Post-Admission RTE Policy

1. Equal Treatment

Children admitted under the RTE quota are treated equally with other students in all aspects of school life. There is no discrimination in seating arrangements, access to facilities, participation in school activities, or any other aspect of education.

2. Financial Support

The trust provides the following support to children admitted under the RTE quota:

  • Free textbooks and workbooks
  • Free school uniform (two sets per academic year)
  • Free midday meal
  • Free stationery kit (notebooks, pencils, erasers, etc.)
  • Free participation in all curricular and co-curricular activities

3. Academic Support

The school provides additional academic support to RTE students, if required, to ensure they can cope with the curriculum and achieve their full potential. This includes:

  • Remedial classes for subjects where students may need extra help
  • Special attention by teachers to address learning gaps
  • Regular parent-teacher meetings to discuss the child's progress

4. No Detention Policy

In accordance with the RTE Act, no child admitted under the RTE quota is detained in any class until the completion of elementary education (up to Class 8). Continuous and Comprehensive Evaluation (CCE) is followed to assess the child's progress.

5. Grievance Redressal

A dedicated RTE Grievance Redressal Cell is established in the school to address any concerns or complaints related to RTE admissions or implementation. Parents/guardians can approach the RTE Coordinator or the Principal with their grievances, which will be resolved promptly.

This RTE policy is subject to revision based on changes in government guidelines and regulations. The school management reserves the right to make necessary amendments to comply with the legal requirements.

For any queries related to RTE admissions, please contact our RTE Coordinator at rte@ssvtrust.com or visit the school office during working hours.
